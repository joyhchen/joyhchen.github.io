The Small Things

The minor details are the ones that count the most yet seem the most trivial. Whether it be one sheet of paper or a bottle of water, every little thing counts. Here are several "small details" that can help make a positive difference in your everyday life.

In the Classroom

If you have homework you don't need to save, try to recycle it instead of throwing it away. You can also consider keeping your past papers and creating a scrapbook with them if you are wary about recycling classwork. When it comes to textbooks, consider creating a paper book cover instead of buying one from the store. Paper book covers are recyclable and often times very capable of protecting the covers of books.

At Home

If you would like to recycle at home, you can apply some principles to your garden. If you have any garden or kitchen waste, try composting, where waste is put in compost and degrades to help fertilize the soil in your garden. If you cut your lawn, consider keeping the clippings on the ground to fertilize instead of throwing them away. Finally, if you are looking to breathe new life into your garden, consider planting a new tree. These three suggestions are great ways to start contributing to the health of the world near the house.

Starting Small

A common question among those who wish to recycle is where items can be recycled. In fact, the inability to find recycling centers is one of the main reasons people choose not to recycle. Some of us have had those times where we can't find the recycling bin, so we toss our paper in the trash instead. In moments like those, throwing recyclable items in the trash is a choice out of convenience. However, the little moments where trash cans are nowhere to be found add up and so does the amount of recyclable items thrown in the trash that could have been recycled.

So, we would like to propose a challenge. The next time you can't find the recycling bin, hold on to your item or go find a bin. While it seems relatively simple, the amount of waste reduced adds up. Without having to travel hundreds of miles, you can contribute to a cleaner Earth.
