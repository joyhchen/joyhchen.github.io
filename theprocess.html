The Process

Nothing's Impossible

While recycling is beneficial, most people are not aware of the processes that the main recyclable materials go through. The following paragraphs explore what specifically happens to materials after they are recycled in order to educate readers about the most common recyclable materials that are tossed in the wrong bin.

Paper

When you recycle a sheet of paper, it is important to know you are making a difference. The paper will be taken to a recycling plant. There it will be pulped. The paper is cut into small pieces and pulped with water. It is then cleansed by removing any unwanted waste. Next, the ink is removed from the paper pulp. In doing so, the pulp is mixed with a surfactant. It is then rinsed in water and bleached. Finally, the construction of the new paper product begins. Essentially the clean pulp is used to form a new product. The new product often ranges from more paper, paper bags, and paper plates The water used in the process is filtered to be used again.

1

The paper is sent to recycling plant.

2

After arriving, it is cut into small pieces and pulped.

3

Once pulped, the paper is cleansed and the ink is removed.

4

Finally, the paper is reused.

Aluminum

The next time you drink a beverage in an aluminum can, consider recycling it or saving it if there isn't a recycling bin in the room. After a can is recycled, it is cleaned of any other substances or materials. After cleaning, the aluminum is sorted along with any other metals and bundled to create another item. Aluminum can be an especially important material to recycle as it doesn't degrade as much as plastic when recycled.

1

With aluminum, the can or scrap is cleaned of any other substances or materials right when it arrives.

2

After cleaning, it is sorted into unique categories.

3

Finally, the aluminum is bundled with its related items to create a new product.

Plastic

Plastic water bottle sales have been going up, leading to more waste that isn't recycled. Plastic is a major issue as often times people put their plastic items in the wrong bin. We encourage you to go with reusable items for water, but if that is not an option, consider recycling your plastic bottles instead of throwing them in the trash.

After a piece of plastic is recycled, it is transported to a facility where it is purified and cleaned. Then, the plastic is broken apart and separated into a group depending on its color and type. After separation, the plastic is shredded, washed, and dried. This ensures a pure piece of plastic that is then used for the manufacturing of another product.

1

Plastic is taken to a facility where it is cleaned and purified.

2

The plastic is separated into separate categories depending on its characteristics.

3

After separation, the plastic is washed and processed to create pure plastic to manufacture a new product.
