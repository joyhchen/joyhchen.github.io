Writing things down to remember

Nov 15, 2022

When I was a kid, I had a Radio Disney DVD. I can’t remember which one anymore, but I do remember Jesse McCartney was on it. I’m about 60% convinced this was my first introduction to both him and the song “Beautiful Soul.” Around the same time, I would burn CDs using the eMachines computer my dad owned. At this point, this is also a blur to me. I vaguely recall burning music from my favorite radio station, 93.9 Lite FM, onto CDs, and I can only really say “Making Love Out of Nothing at All” by Air Supply was definitely on one of these CDs. “Right Here Waiting” by Richard Marx was also likely on one of them.

My dad owned a 500GB portable hard drive, mostly used for storing music, with some real bops like “Because I’m a Girl” by Kiss and the Japanese version of “No. 1” by BoA. My dad also eventually bought me my own 17" HP Pavillion laptop. I did a number of things on this laptop, most notably:

  • Downloaded viruses multiple times
  • Learned how to reset my computer (multiple times)
  • Spent many hours after dinner playing Roblox (back when it was just a desktop app)
  • Tried learning how to program Roblox games, but I couldn’t figure out Lua
  • Played MapleStory
  • Browsed a lot of internet forums
  • Had a Blogspot dedicated to some mix of the games I played

These days, I no longer play games. You could say I make a living writing code. Also, anti-virus protections have come a long way. I’m old enough to remember:

  • Burning CDs (as previously mentioned)
  • What a VCR player is (I owned one!)
  • PHP forum software

And maybe this list is ridiculous to somoene.
