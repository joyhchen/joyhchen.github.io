Volunteer

Are you interested in helping out a recycling cause? There are a variety of ways you can help out the community. In Illinois, you can help out various organizations, including Keep Northern Illinois Beautiful which you can learn more about at http://www.knib.org/get-involved/volunteer/.
Opportunities include offering presentations to young children and adults, collecting recyclable materials, picking up trash, fundraising, delivering clothing, and helping out with paperwork at the KNIB office. Many of these activities apply locally as well if you live in another region of Illinois or the United States. If you are looking for community service hours, many organizations will sign off on them as well. Volunteer your time to making the Earth a more beautiful home!


Helpful Organizations

These organizations (both local and national) may be able to offer more volunteer opportunities if you are looking to help out in the community. The larger organizations will most likely have opportunities in your area, and if they do not, feel free to form a chapter at your school or business and contact the organization!

  • Illinois Recycles
  • Keep Northern Illinois Beautiful
  • Keep America Beautiful
  • National Recycling Coalition
  • National Waste and Recycling Association

While we don't guarantee these organizations will help you out, you can always try to contact them. If they do not respond, consider starting your own club or organization separate from being a chapter of one of the organizations listed above.

There are a variety of ideas that can kick your volunteering off, including but not limited to

  • Cash for Cans - Collect aluminum cans for money or offer your own money for other kids to collect aluminum cans. Cans that otherwise would have been thrown away will be recycled.
  • Class Recycling Competitions - Have different classes or grades in a school compete to see who can recycle the most.
  • Craft Competitions - Consider using recycled items to create different pieces of art.
  • Engineering Competitions - Instead of creating pieces of art, give students or community members the option to design a beneficial project such as a birdhouse out of recycled items.

If you like any of the ideas, feel free to build off of them and create bigger community events. As long as you are increasing awareness about recycling, these events can be fun and educational ways to positively impact the environment.
