Year of Books Challenge

Even though Mark Zuckerberg announced this challenge as his goal for 2015, I think his mission is universally applicable. A couple days ago I was browsing a Reddit thread for a Chinese documentary about factory life for migrant workers when I saw a book recommendation in the comments. A user had mentioned that Leslie T. Chang wrote an interesting book about the issue titled Factory Girls. Since spring break had just started for me and I had nothing else to do, I decided to read this book. Reading again made me realize how much I missed picking up a book I actually wanted to read. So, I resolved to start reading again, but I'm tweaking Mark Zuckerberg's rules a bit. He mentioned reading a book every other week, amounting to 26 books in a 52-week year. My goal, like his, is to read 26 books this year (12 months starting from today March 27, 2016) but in a looser time frame, meaning I won't limit myself to every other week.

Mark Zuckerberg's primary goal was to increase his awareness of the world through exposure to various cultures, languages, and experiences in general. While I am also interested in increasing my consciousness of where I stand in the world, I hope that reading can contribute to my development as a writer as well.

The problem with long commitments like this is that it's hard to stay focused. I think the primary reason my development has been slow is the fact that I'm easily distracted. It's easy to jump on to the newest technology and abandon it a week later. So, I guess the last thing I hope this challenge can teach me is discipline. Making a plan and sticking to the course appears simple yet when put in action feels tiring.
