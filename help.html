Help Out

Get Out There

You can help out the community in many ways. If you want to encourage recycling, there's always someone who wants to listen. In fact, most people know it's beneficial to recycle, but it is hard for them to get accurate, helpful information.

You can do something as simple as posting on Instagram or Twitter to spread the message about recycling. If you want to do something more substantial, you can help out at a community center or organize a drive at your school. See the "Volunteer" and "Organize a Drive" pages for more information on how you can help out your community today!


Social Media Post Ideas

  • Facebook - Facebook is a great way to connect with friends and family, and being more close-knit, you can share some of your own experiences with recycling. For example, a photo and an anecdote in the caption that details how you recycled and how you impacted the world. Often times these personal experiences can help people imagine what they could do too!
  • Twitter - With a more limited amount of characters, you may not be able to share an entire story, but what you can do is share a small snippet of recycling in your life. Some of the biggest statements are made in the fewest words.
  • Instagram - Pictures being the main focus, you can upload a picture of what you recycled and explain why recycling matters. It doesn't have to be a grand story, but it can be a simple reminder to friends and family that recycling is all around us. If you can't think about anything you recycled, simply taking a picture of a recycling bin and sharing your thoughts on recycling can be a reminder about recycling to those who otherwise wouldn't have recycled.
  • Pinterest - Pinterest is unique in that it is an image-board website. You can keep a board of recycling-related items and add to create a cohesive board that encompasses all aspects of recycling. Anyone stumbling upon it may be intrigued and look further into it.
  • Reddit - Reddit is a great place to find niche topics and talk with others with similar interests. If you would like to share your experiences, there are subreddits like /r/recycle and /r/recycling. Subreddits like /r/upcycling are places where you can find out how to turn everyday items into more useful utilities.
  • Tumblr - Tumblr is another community where you can find more people interested in recycling and its effects on the environment. You can find blogs to follow, post your own pictures and stories, and share ideas about recycling.
  • Other - Every social media network has a way for you to get the word out about recycling. As long as the post is not hostile, we encourage you to find your own methods of educating others about recycling.

Attempting to Reach Out

While you are not going to reach all your followers, friends, or family, at least one person will be reminded to recycle, and all it takes is one person. If you don't like any of our suggestions, feel free to come up with your own and tell us how you make your best effort to #recycle! If you are on Twitter, Facebook, or Instagram, remember to tag us in your post. We are always looking for ways to spread the word about recycling.

At the end of every page is a "Spread the Message" banner for easy access to our social media accounts where you can contact us. We encourage you to take advantage of the feature and spread the message about recycling with us. Together we can make the world a more beautiful place one action at a time.
