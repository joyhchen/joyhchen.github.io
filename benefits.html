Benefits

You're Making a Difference

It can be hard to imagine how simply tossing an inanimate object in a bin can help contribute to the Earth's wellbeing, but there are so many ways recycling can help maintain our land so that we can continue to laugh, run, and explore.

You are conserving energy.

Products made with recycled materials take less energy to make. For example, the production of recycled paper consumes 40% less energy than the production of paper from unprocessed wood. Similarly, the recycling of one glass bottle conserves enough energy to keep a 100 watt light bulb awake for four hours. Every recycled item can contribute to the conservation of more energy. The amount of energy one recycled aluminum can saves is astonishing. With millions of recycled cans, we can save even more energy.


You are conserving resources.

Recycling also ensures that our planet's natural resources are depleted less quickly. For every one ton of glass recycled, 1300 lbs of sand, 410 lbs of soda ash, and 380 lbs of limestone are saved. For every ton of paper produced, using recycled paper instead of raw material saves over 7000 gallons of water. Across the board, the statistics show that we utilize less resources when we produce products with recycled materials instead of raw.


You are reducing waste.

When we use less resources, we also produce less waste. The production of many household items is a long process that leaves many leftover scraps that negatively impact the world. By reducing the number of scraps we use, we are also producing less waste. In fact, one ton of non-recycled office paper takes 24 trees to produce. The sawing down of all these trees only produces more waste that is not recycled. By recycling, we bypass the process of cutting down so many trees, resulting in less waste.


Your recyclables are used to create tangible products.

When you recycle a sheet of paper, you're contributing to the production of another sheet of paper that may sit in front of you another day. Instead of ending up in landfills, your recyclable materials are used to create new products. If you see a green recycling logo on your next notebook, be reminded that that notebook was manufactured from recycled material, and it might have been the very material that you recycled.


You are contributing to the economy.

The amount of steel thrown away in 2009 in the United States was worth more than $3 billion. In the same year, plastic worth $485 million was thrown away. By recycling, your materials can be used efficiently and beneficially instead of being wasted. Not only will recycling help our environment, but it will also contribute to the economy and save natural resources.

Recycling is so much more than Recycling

It takes one second to recycle. In that same one second, you are changing the world. If you have the opportunity to recycle a piece of paper, consider taking the time to recycle it instead of tossing it in the trash. Your sheet of paper can contribute to keeping a healthy planet, reducing the massive landfills in our world, and growing our economy. The benefits of recycling are worth taking the time to find a recycling bin.
